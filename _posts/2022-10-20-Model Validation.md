### Progressive overload and model validation

Progressive overload underpins individual success in virtually every sport, and many domains besides. But many organizations don't seem to use this principle to drive development. 

A key aspect of progressive overload is having a definition of failure -  without this, it's not possible to define what's meant by 'overload'. Testing for failure = validation. How can a decision tool be validated?

- Validate in development against historical data.
- Validate in production via impact.
- Validate using logic/via reference to existing models.

Crucially, whatever you do - validate!

Why do organizations not do this?

Progressive overload necessarily involves failure, which is uncomfortable and stressful.
